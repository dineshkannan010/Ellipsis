## Bursting into tears, Ventura says she'd give back $20 million settlement if she never had to do "Freak Offs"
Prosecutor Emily Johnson is asking Cassie Ventura about the lawsuit she [settled with Sean “Diddy” Combs in 2023](https://www.cnn.com/2023/11/17/entertainment/sean-diddy-combs-settles-lawsuit-cassie). Ventura testified that she got $20 million in that settlement. 
Johnson asked Ventura if she’d give the money back if it meant she never had to endure “Freak Offs.” 
“I’d give that money back if I never had to have ‘Freak Offs,’” Ventura said. 
Bursting into tears, Ventura continued, “If I never had to have ‘Freak Offs,’ I would have had agency and autonomy.” 
Audibly upset, Ventura said, “And I wouldn’t have had to work so hard to get it back.”

## Ventura testifies there were times when Combs was kind, but those periods didn't last

## There’s a tense atmosphere outside the courthouse, where crowds of media and onlookers have gathered
From CNN's Alli Rosenbloom 
A tense scene has been unfolding this week outside the downtown Manhattan courthouse where Sean “Diddy” Combs is standing trial on sex trafficking and racketeering conspiracy charges. 
CNN’s Elizabeth Wagmeister, who has been reporting from the courthouse throughout the first week of the federal criminal trial, said Friday that some fans of Combs have gathered outside to voice support for him.
